Story

"How does AI learn like humans?"

My academic journey began with this fundamental question. While leading the academic club 'BAMBOO', I explored the principles of AI with my peers. Through various projects, I realized that many AI methodologies mimic the principles of nature and life, much like how the human visual cortex inspired CNNs.

This insight led me to Brain Decoding. I am convinced that a deep understanding of the brain will be the breakthrough for next-generation AI. I am now dedicated to realizing the "AI-to-Brain" paradigm, where we not only mimic the brain to improve AI but also use advanced AI models to understand the human brain.

Interests

Focus

Research Interests

My research goal is to internalize the brain's computational principles into AI models and use them to explore the brain again.

Focus

1 - Brain-Inspired AI

Developing AI architectures inspired by neural mechanisms, such as the dual-stream visual pathways (ventral/dorsal) and hierarchical processing, to enhance model robustness and interpretability.

2 - Brain-Computer Interface (BCI)

Decoding human intentions and visual experiences from non-invasive signals (EEG, fMRI). I aim to build practical BCI systems that can reconstruct visual stimuli or detect emotional states.

3 - Multi-modal Learning

Integrating various modalities—such as vision, language, and physiological signals—to create more comprehensive AI models. Examples include Text-to-3D generation and EEG-fMRI fusion.

Research Vision

Vision

"From Brain-to-AI, Towards AI-to-Brain"

I aim to go beyond simply creating AI that mimics the brain.
My ultimate goal is to use highly advanced AI models as a 'microscope' or 'simulator'
to inversely explore the human brain, formulating new scientific questions and finding answers.
